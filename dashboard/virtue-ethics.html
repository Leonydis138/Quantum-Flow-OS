The Golden Mean Axiom

Unlike rule-based rules or outcome math, Virtue Ethics evaluates actions by the disposition they cultivate. Virtue (arete) is formed through habitual repetition (hexis). For every virtue, there is a balanced Golden Mean flanked by two vices of imbalance:

Deficiency (Vice of Defect) Too little manifestation of a trait. Cowardice, dishonesty, or calloused indifference.
Excess (Vice of Excess) Too much of a trait, turning it destructive. Recklessness, over-caution, or punitive rigidity.
